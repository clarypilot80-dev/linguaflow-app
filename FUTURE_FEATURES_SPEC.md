Application Roadmap: Progression Framework & AI Analyzer
1. The "Structural Core" Progression Model
The application's curriculum will move away from isolated vocabulary and focus on teaching "Conversational Bridges." The goal is to equip the user with the structural load-bearing columns of the target language, allowing them to dynamically fill in the blanks with their own vocabulary.

Phase 1: The Control Mechanisms (Emergency Brakes)

Concept: Phrases that control the flow, pace, and input of the native speaker.

Examples: "Could you say that slower?", "I don't understand that word."

Purpose: Eliminates conversational panic. Allows the user to learn new words in real-time by asking the speaker for clarification.

Phase 2: The Logical Connectors

Concept: Words that establish cause, effect, and contrast to link two simple ideas into a complex thought.

Examples: "Because of...", "Which means...", "Instead of..."

Purpose: Elevates the user from speaking in fragmented "caveman" sentences to demonstrating intellectual flow.

Phase 3: The Topic Expanders

Concept: Phrases designed to pivot the conversation and maintain engagement.

Examples: "Speaking of that...", "On a different note..."

Purpose: Prevents dead ends and allows the user to steer the conversation toward topics where their vocabulary is strongest.

2. The "Bridge Builder" Island Format
UI/UX Concept: A new type of learning module.

Mechanic: The user is presented with two simple concepts in the target language. Instead of translating them, the user must use Active Recall to verbally provide the correct "Bridge" (from the phases above) to logically connect the two ideas.

3. Future Power Feature: Real-World Conversation Analyzer
Concept: An audio/video upload tool for analyzing genuine, real-world interactions (e.g., recording a quick interaction at a café or asking for directions upon arriving in Paris).

Mechanic: The user records a real conversation. The app's backend AI processes the audio and provides a post-conversation breakdown.

Deliverables:

Transcription: Accurate text of what both the user and the native speaker said.

Bridge Grading: Identifies missed opportunities where the user could have used a Phase 1, 2, or 3 bridge to improve the flow.

Pacing & Pronunciation: Analyzes the user's delivery and suggests corrections for clarity.
